Proposed methodology and novelty review

An eight-cohort, multimodal swept-source OCTA framework for the RASTA dataset, with a conservative assessment of what is established, what may be new, and what cannot yet be claimed.

Novelty is not established for the broad concept.

Closely related work exists on the same RASTA dataset, on multimodal OCTA plus clinical CAD prediction, on three-layer OCTA fusion, and on bilateral contrastive pretraining. The defensible contribution is the exact integrated design and its evaluation, if the experiments support it.

Scope and scientific endpoint

This distinction controls every valid conclusion.

This draft describes the proposed full Version 1 pipeline. In the active eight-class expansion, Phases 1 to 3 are implemented. Downstream Phases 4 onward must still be reconciled with the eight-class artifacts before training. Historical four-class outputs must remain untouched.

The current supervised label is cohort identity, not a direct CAD diagnosis.

The target is the source folder: 1_RETINORM, 2_ORNET, 3_FAMILIPO, 4_MRCC, 5_GIANTS, 6_AWARD, 7_EVIRED, or 8_ORNET_TEMOINS. Only MRCC is directly tied to coronary revascularization. The remaining groups represent controls or other study populations. Therefore, the present model can be described as eight-cohort discrimination from OCTA and clinical data. It cannot be described as binary CAD prediction unless a separately validated patient-level CAD endpoint is supplied.

This is more than a wording issue. Because cohort identity is also study identity, the model may learn age, diabetes status, acquisition history, or other cohort-specific selection effects. Clinical variables can make this especially easy. High cohort-classification accuracy would not establish that the model detects CAD or a general cardiovascular phenotype.

Proposed research questions

  1. Can layer-specific SVC, DVC, and CC OCTA representations discriminate the eight RASTA cohorts under patient-grouped evaluation?
  2. Does explicit modeling of interactions among retinal depths improve macro-F1 and balanced accuracy over single-layer and direct-concatenation baselines?
  3. Do clinical variables, generated masks, and generated OCTA biomarkers add information beyond raw OCTA images?
  4. Are the resulting probabilities calibrated, and can uncertainty identify unreliable cases?
  5. Do any observed gains remain after age, sex, diabetes, and study-cohort confounding are challenged through sensitivity analyses?

Biological and clinical precedent

Independent clinical studies support the underlying biological premise, but do not prove that this model will generalize. OCTA vessel-density and perfusion alterations have been reported in coronary heart disease, non-obstructive and obstructive CAD, and coronary stenosis cohorts. Examples include a 2019 four-layer OCTA study, a 2023 CAD subtype study, a 2025 study of 747 participants, and a 2026 coronary-stenosis biomarker study.

Conservative novelty verdict

No “first” claim is currently safe.

No exact duplicate of the complete proposed pipeline was located, but every major ingredient has close precedent.

The publishable contribution should be framed as the design and rigorous evaluation of a specific integrated framework, not the invention of OCTA cardiovascular prediction, multimodal fusion, bilateral contrastive learning, mask-derived biomarkers, prototypes, or uncertainty estimation.

What could become genuinely strong

  • A clinically validated CAD endpoint independent of cohort folder and study membership.
  • External validation on a different center, device, or cardiovascular cohort.
  • A prospective comparison against the published RASTA EfficientNetV2-B3 approach.
  • Evidence that cross-layer attention, bilateral OCTA pretraining, and generated-mask pathways improve performance under matched ablations.
  • Demonstrated robustness after controlling for age, sex, diabetes, image quality, and cohort-selection effects.
  • A manually annotated mask subset for independent evaluation of pseudo-mask and student-mask quality.

Proposed methodology

Formal specification of the complete eight-cohort Version 1 study.

Three OCTA layers→fold-local SimCLR→layer-aware attention→clinical and biomarker fusion→mask branch→prototype classifier→calibration and uncertainty

1. Study units, labels, and notation

Let (i \in \{1,\ldots,N\}) index patients, (e \in \{OD,OS\}) index eyes, and (\ell \in \{S,D,C\}) index superficial, deep, and choriocapillaris layers. Each eye contributes images (x_{i,e}^{\ell} \in \mathbb{R}^{H\times W}), a patient-level clinical vector (c_i \in \mathbb{R}^{D_c}), a missingness mask (r_i \in \{0,1\}^{D_c}), and cohort label (y_i \in \{0,\ldots,7\}).

All eyes from one patient must remain in the same outer fold. The eye may be treated as a training sample, but the patient is the independent unit for final inference and confidence intervals.

2. Image preprocessing

Unreadable, zero-variance, all-black, low-dynamic-range, and low-quality scans are flagged before modeling. For image (x), percentile normalization uses the second and ninety-eighth percentiles (q_2(x)) and (q_{98}(x)):

\[ x^{(p)} = \operatorname{clip}\left(\frac{x-q_2(x)}{q_{98}(x)-q_2(x)+\epsilon},0,1\right). \]

CLAHE is applied independently by layer, followed by bicubic resizing to (224\times224). Fold-specific standardization uses only outer-training patients:

\[ \hat{x}_{i,e}^{\ell}=\frac{x_{i,e}^{(p),\ell}-\mu_{\ell,f}}{\sigma_{\ell,f}+\epsilon}, \qquad \mu_{\ell,f},\sigma_{\ell,f}\text{ estimated on training fold }f. \]

3. Clinical data and missingness

Continuous clinical variables are standardized with training-fold statistics. For feature (j), observed value (c_{ij}), observation indicator (r_{ij}), and learned missing value (a_j), the model input is

\[ \tilde c_{ij}=r_{ij}\frac{c_{ij}-\mu_{j,f}}{\sigma_{j,f}+\epsilon}+(1-r_{ij})a_j. \]

Binary variables retain their encoded value. Ordinal variables use learned embeddings. During training, 10% of observed clinical values are additionally masked to improve missingness robustness. Generated biomarkers are normalized separately and receive an explicit QC/missingness indicator.

4. Fold-local, layer-specific contrastive pretraining

Three independent ConvNeXt-Tiny encoders are pretrained, one per OCTA layer. For bilateral patients, the positive pair is the same layer from OD and OS. For unilateral patients, the pair consists of two conservative augmentations of the same image. Pretraining is repeated inside each outer training fold to prevent held-out patient information from entering the representation.

For two views (v_i,v_j), encoder (f_{\theta_\ell}), projection head (g_{\phi_\ell}), and normalized projection (z),

\[ z_i=\frac{g_{\phi_\ell}(f_{\theta_\ell}(v_i))}{\|g_{\phi_\ell}(f_{\theta_\ell}(v_i))\|_2}, \qquad s_{ik}=z_i^\top z_k. \]

The directional NT-Xent loss with temperature (\tau=0.07) is

\[ \ell_{i,j}=-\log\frac{\exp(s_{ij}/\tau)}{\sum_{k=1}^{2B}\mathbb{1}_{[k\ne i]}\exp(s_{ik}/\tau)}, \qquad \mathcal L_{\mathrm{NTX}}=\frac{1}{2B}\sum_{(i,j)}\ell_{i,j}. \]

The expanded run uses 120 epochs, SGD with momentum 0.9, weight decay (10^{-4}), initial learning rate 0.03 with cosine decay, effective batch size 256, gradient clipping at 1.0, and no AMP for this pretraining stage.

Bilateral positives require a sensitivity analysis.

Contralateral eyes share systemic state but are not anatomically identical and may have asymmetric pathology or artifacts. The study must compare bilateral positives against standard same-image SimCLR and report whether the bilateral strategy helps each layer.

5. Layer-specific downstream encoders

After SimCLR, each projection head is discarded. The three independent backbones produce final feature maps (U^\ell\in\mathbb{R}^{7\times7\times768}), retained for Grad-CAM. Global average pooling and a layer-specific projection produce a 256-dimensional token:

\[ u^\ell=\operatorname{GAP}(U^\ell),\qquad f^\ell=\operatorname{LN}\left(W_2^\ell\operatorname{GELU}(W_1^\ell u^\ell+b_1^\ell)+b_2^\ell\right)\in\mathbb{R}^{256}. \]

6. Identity-aware cross-layer attention

Learned layer-identity embeddings (e^S,e^D,e^C\in\mathbb{R}^{256}) preserve retinal-depth identity:

\[ T=\begin{bmatrix}f^S+e^S\\f^D+e^D\\f^C+e^C\end{bmatrix}\in\mathbb{R}^{3\times256}. \]

For attention head (h\in\{1,\ldots,8\}), with head dimension (d_h=32),

\[ Q_h=\operatorname{LN}(T)W_h^Q,\quad K_h=\operatorname{LN}(T)W_h^K,\quad V_h=\operatorname{LN}(T)W_h^V, \] \[ A_h=\operatorname{softmax}\left(\frac{Q_hK_h^\top}{\sqrt{d_h}}\right),\qquad O=\operatorname{Concat}_{h=1}^{8}(A_hV_h)W^O. \] \[ T'=T+\operatorname{Dropout}(O),\qquad H=T'+\operatorname{Dropout}\left(W_2\operatorname{GELU}(W_1\operatorname{LN}(T'))\right), \] \[ f_{\mathrm{octa}}=\frac{1}{3}\sum_{\ell\in\{S,D,C\}}H_\ell\in\mathbb{R}^{256}. \]

The feed-forward width is 1024 and dropout is 0.1.

7. Weak segmentation and generated biomarkers

Classical image processing generates SVC and DVC vessel masks, SVC and DVC FAZ masks, and a CC flow-deficit mask. These are pseudo-labels, not human ground truth. After montage and quantitative QC, a U-Net++ student is trained against QC-passed pseudo-labels and then frozen. The downstream operational source is the frozen student output.

For binary target (y_p\in\{0,1\}), probability (p_p\), and pixel set (\Omega),

\[ \operatorname{Dice}=\frac{2\sum_{p\in\Omega}p_py_p+\epsilon}{\sum_{p\in\Omega}p_p+\sum_{p\in\Omega}y_p+\epsilon}, \qquad \mathcal L_{\mathrm{Dice}}=1-\operatorname{Dice}. \] \[ \operatorname{TI}=\frac{TP}{TP+\alpha_{tv}FP+\beta_{tv}FN+\epsilon}, \qquad \mathcal L_{\mathrm{Tversky}}=1-\operatorname{TI},\quad \beta_{tv}=0.7. \] \[ \mathcal L_{\mathrm{FocalSeg}}=-\frac{1}{|\Omega|}\sum_{p\in\Omega} \left[y_p(1-p_p)^\gamma\log p_p+(1-y_p)p_p^\gamma\log(1-p_p)\right],\quad\gamma=2. \] \[ \mathcal L_{\mathrm{student}}=\alpha_s\mathcal L_{\mathrm{Tversky}}+(1-\alpha_s)\mathcal L_{\mathrm{FocalSeg}}+\lambda_d\mathcal L_{\mathrm{Dice}}. \]

For binary vessel mask (M), skeleton (S(M)), FAZ region (F), CC deficit mask (D), pixel area (a_{px}), and image domain (\Omega), generated biomarkers include

\[ \operatorname{VD}=\frac{\sum_{p\in\Omega}M_p}{|\Omega|},\qquad \operatorname{VLD}=\frac{\sum_{p\in\Omega}S(M)_p}{|\Omega|}, \] \[ A_{FAZ}=a_{px}|F|,\qquad C_{FAZ}=\frac{4\pi A_{FAZ}}{P_{FAZ}^2+\epsilon}, \] \[ d_{FAZ}=\frac{\|\bar p_F-p_{center}\|_2}{\sqrt{H^2+W^2}},\qquad \operatorname{FD\%}=100\frac{\sum_{p\in\Omega}D_p}{|\Omega|}. \]

Flow-deficit count is the number of retained connected components. Mean and median component areas are computed after the same physical pixel-area conversion.

The five masks are stacked and encoded:

\[ M_{all}=\operatorname{Stack}(M_S,M_D,F_S,F_D,D_C)\in\mathbb{R}^{5\times224\times224}, \qquad f_{mask}=E_m(M_{all})\in\mathbb{R}^{128}. \]

8. Tabular encoder and multimodal fusion

Let (b\in\mathbb{R}^{D_b}) be normalized generated biomarkers. The tabular vector is (q=[\tilde c;b]\). The encoder is

\[ h_1=\operatorname{Drop}_{0.3}(\operatorname{GELU}(W_1\operatorname{LN}(q)+b_1)), \] \[ f_{tab}=\operatorname{Drop}_{0.2}(\operatorname{LN}(\operatorname{GELU}(W_2h_1+b_2)))\in\mathbb{R}^{256}. \]

The final compatibility fusion uses each modality once:

\[ f_{cond}=\operatorname{LN}(f_{octa}+W_tf_{tab}+b_t), \] \[ h=\operatorname{LN}\left(\operatorname{GELU}\left(W_f[f_{cond};f_{mask}]+b_f\right)\right)\in\mathbb{R}^{512}. \]

The earlier one-token tabular cross-attention is not called attention in this draft because a single key-value token yields a softmax weight of one. It is mathematically residual conditioning.

9. Prototype-augmented cohort classifier

The standard head produces penultimate embedding (z\in\mathbb{R}^{128}) and parametric logits (s_k):

\[ z=\operatorname{Drop}_{0.3}\left(\operatorname{GELU}\left(W_2\operatorname{Drop}_{0.3}(\operatorname{GELU}(W_1h+b_1))+b_2\right)\right), \qquad s=W_3z+b_3. \]

For class (k), the batch mean embedding and EMA prototype are

\[ \bar z_k^{(t)}=\frac{1}{|B_k|}\sum_{i\in B_k}z_i,\qquad P_k^{(t)}=\rho P_k^{(t-1)}+(1-\rho)\bar z_k^{(t)}. \]

Prototype and combined logits are

\[ r_k=-\frac{\|z-P_k\|_2^2}{\tau_p},\quad \tau_p=0.1, \qquad o_k=s_k+\operatorname{softplus}(a)r_k, \] \[ p_k=\frac{\exp(o_k)}{\sum_{j=1}^{8}\exp(o_j)}. \]

10. Classification objective

With label-smoothing parameter (\varepsilon=0.1), the target distribution is

\[ \tilde y_{ik}=(1-\varepsilon)\mathbb{1}[y_i=k]+\frac{\varepsilon}{K},\qquad K=8. \]

Training-fold class weights use normalized inverse frequency, (w_k=(1/n_k)/(\sum_j1/n_j)\cdot K). The default classifier loss is

\[ \mathcal L_{cls}=-\frac{1}{B}\sum_{i=1}^{B}\sum_{k=1}^{K}w_k\tilde y_{ik}\log(p_{ik}+\epsilon). \]

If the training-fold imbalance ratio exceeds 10, focal cross-entropy is compared:

\[ \mathcal L_{focal}=-\frac{1}{B}\sum_{i=1}^{B}\sum_{k=1}^{K}\tilde y_{ik}(1-p_{ik})^\gamma\log(p_{ik}+\epsilon),\qquad\gamma=2. \]

The frozen student is excluded from the default disease-classifier objective, so (\mathcal L_{total}=\mathcal L_{cls}). A joint segmentation-classification objective is experimental only.

11. Training schedule

Stage 1
120-epoch, fold-local, independent SimCLR pretraining for SVC, DVC, and CC.
Stage 2
Freeze image backbones for 15 epochs. Train projection heads, tabular and mask encoders, cross-layer attention, fusion, prototype state, and classifier at learning rate (3\times10^{-4}).
Stage 3
Fine-tune for at most 100 epochs using AdamW. Backbone learning rate is (3\times10^{-5}); downstream learning rate is (3\times10^{-4}); weight decay is (10^{-2}). Use five warmup epochs, cosine annealing, and early stopping with patience 15 on inner-validation macro-F1.

Synchronized geometric augmentation is applied to all three OCTA layers from the same eye. Validation and outer-test images are never augmented.

Evaluation and statistical analysis

Designed around patient independence and out-of-fold predictions.

Nested patient-grouped evaluation

Use the fixed five patient-grouped folds as outer test folds. Within each outer-training set, form a patient-grouped 80/20 inner split for early stopping and temperature fitting. No outer-test patient may influence preprocessing statistics, contrastive pretraining, pseudo-label model selection, feature selection, early stopping, or calibration.

For a patient with eye set (E_i), the patient-level probability is

\[ \bar p_{ik}=\frac{1}{|E_i|}\sum_{e\in E_i}p_{i,e,k}. \]

Calibration and MC-dropout uncertainty

Temperature (T>0) is fitted on inner-validation logits by minimizing negative log-likelihood:

\[ T^*=\arg\min_{T>0}-\sum_i\log\operatorname{softmax}(o_i/T)_{y_i}. \]

With (M=30) stochastic dropout passes,

\[ \bar p_k=\frac{1}{M}\sum_{m=1}^{M}p_k^{(m)},\qquad \widehat{\operatorname{Var}}(p_k)=\frac{1}{M-1}\sum_{m=1}^{M}(p_k^{(m)}-\bar p_k)^2, \] \[ H(\bar p)=-\sum_{k=1}^{K}\bar p_k\log(\bar p_k+\epsilon). \]

The entropy threshold must be selected on inner validation data. Outer-test data are used only once for final coverage and risk reporting.

Classification, calibration, and uncertainty metrics

\[ \operatorname{Sensitivity}_k=\frac{TP_k}{TP_k+FN_k},\qquad \operatorname{Specificity}_k=\frac{TN_k}{TN_k+FP_k}, \] \[ \operatorname{Precision}_k=\frac{TP_k}{TP_k+FP_k},\qquad F1_k=\frac{2\operatorname{Precision}_k\operatorname{Sensitivity}_k}{\operatorname{Precision}_k+\operatorname{Sensitivity}_k}, \] \[ \operatorname{MacroF1}=\frac{1}{K}\sum_{k=1}^{K}F1_k,\qquad \operatorname{BalancedAccuracy}=\frac{1}{K}\sum_{k=1}^{K}\operatorname{Sensitivity}_k. \]

For (G) confidence bins (B_g),

\[ \operatorname{ECE}=\sum_{g=1}^{G}\frac{|B_g|}{N}\left|\operatorname{acc}(B_g)-\operatorname{conf}(B_g)\right|, \] \[ \operatorname{Brier}=\frac{1}{N}\sum_{i=1}^{N}\sum_{k=1}^{K}(p_{ik}-\mathbb{1}[y_i=k])^2. \]

Report macro one-vs-rest ROC-AUC, macro PR-AUC, macro-F1, balanced accuracy, weighted F1, per-class sensitivity and specificity, normalized confusion matrices, top-2 accuracy, ECE, Brier score, reliability plots, entropy-stratified accuracy, and risk-coverage curves.

Confidence intervals and paired comparisons

Use 1,000 patient-level stratified bootstrap samples. If (\hat\theta^{*(b)}) is the metric in bootstrap sample (b), the percentile interval is

\[ CI_{95\%}=\left[Q_{0.025}(\{\hat\theta^{*(b)}\}),Q_{0.975}(\{\hat\theta^{*(b)}\})\right]. \]

For paired correctness disagreements (n_{01}) and (n_{10}), the continuity-corrected McNemar statistic is

\[ \chi^2=\frac{(|n_{01}-n_{10}|-1)^2}{n_{01}+n_{10}}. \]

Use DeLong tests for paired AUC comparisons and correct (m) confirmatory comparisons by (\alpha_{adj}=\alpha/m). Report effect sizes and confidence intervals, not p-values alone.

Mandatory baselines and ablations

  • Published-style RASTA EfficientNetV2-B3 image baseline.
  • Clinical-only logistic regression, random forest, and XGBoost.
  • Single-layer SVC, DVC, and CC models.
  • Three layers stacked as channels and three independent encoders with direct concatenation.
  • No SimCLR, standard same-image SimCLR, and bilateral-positive SimCLR.
  • No layer identities and no cross-layer attention.
  • Images only, images plus clinical data, images plus masks, and the full model.
  • Classical pseudo-masks versus frozen U-Net++ student masks.
  • No generated biomarkers and no prototype logits.
  • Uncalibrated, temperature-scaled, deterministic, and MC-dropout inference.

Confounding and leakage challenges

  • Report how well a clinical-only model predicts cohort. Strong performance indicates that cohort selection, not retinal morphology alone, drives the target.
  • Repeat image-only analyses after age and sex matching or weighting where sample size permits.
  • Quantify class separability using image-quality measures and acquisition metadata.
  • Do not include variables that deterministically define a proposed clinical endpoint. For example, predicting CHA2DS2-VASc while feeding its component variables is target leakage.
  • For any future CAD analysis, define CAD independently and include CAD-positive and CAD-negative patients across more than one source cohort.

Explainability

For feature map (A^r) and class logit (o_c), Grad-CAM uses

\[ \alpha_r^c=\frac{1}{Z}\sum_{u,v}\frac{\partial o_c}{\partial A_{uv}^r},\qquad L_{\mathrm{GradCAM}}^c=\operatorname{ReLU}\left(\sum_r\alpha_r^cA^r\right). \]

For attention matrix (A\in\mathbb{R}^{3\times3}), layer dominance is

\[ d_k=\frac{1}{3}\sum_{q=1}^{3}A_{qk},\qquad k\in\{S,D,C\}. \]

Grad-CAM, attention dominance, and SHAP are descriptive aids. They do not establish causal anatomy. Their interpretations must agree with ablation results and be reviewed for cohort-specific artifacts.

Publication-ready claim boundaries

Language that remains defensible under peer review.

Claims supported by the proposed design, if results are positive

  • The framework discriminates eight RASTA source cohorts under patient-grouped out-of-fold evaluation.
  • Layer-aware fusion adds measurable value over specified single-layer and simple-fusion baselines.
  • Clinical, mask, biomarker, and prototype branches contribute incremental value demonstrated by matched ablations.
  • Post-hoc calibration improves held-out probability calibration.
  • Uncertainty supports selective review if test error rises monotonically as coverage increases.

Claims that are not currently supported

  • “First use of RASTA for cardiovascular prediction.”
  • “First bilateral-eye contrastive learning method.”
  • “First multimodal OCTA plus clinical CAD model.”
  • “First three-layer OCTA fusion method.”
  • “First prototype, missing-token, segmentation, or uncertainty-aware retinal classifier.”
  • “The model predicts CAD,” while the target remains folder-derived cohort identity.
  • “Attention proves which retinal layer caused the diagnosis.”
  • Clinical deployment, screening utility, or generalizability without external validation.
Recommended manuscript contribution statement

“We propose and evaluate a patient-grouped, layer-aware multimodal framework for eight-cohort classification in the RASTA swept-source OCTA dataset. The method combines fold-local layer-specific contrastive adaptation, identity-aware cross-layer attention, clinical and generated-biomarker conditioning, a quality-controlled weak-segmentation branch, prototype-augmented classification, and calibrated uncertainty. Rather than claiming novelty for the individual components, we test whether their integration yields robust incremental value over published-style and modality-specific baselines.”

Decision required before a CAD manuscript

If the intended paper is truly about coronary artery disease, obtain or derive a clinically adjudicated CAD endpoint that is not synonymous with MRCC membership. Specify the reference standard, such as invasive coronary angiography, coronary CT angiography, documented prior myocardial infarction, or a prespecified composite. Then redesign labels, inclusion criteria, metrics, and baselines around that endpoint. Without this step, the honest paper is about RASTA cohort classification and representation learning, not CAD diagnosis.
